## Yanited 5-2 Ginge FC — Gameweek 8 Report

KSI's **Yanited** strengthened their grip on top spot with a dominant 5-2 victory over an unfortunate **Ginge FC** at Baller Arena on Saturday evening. The result sends Yanited four points clear at the summit with just three gameweeks remaining in the regular season.

## First Half

Yanited were ruthless from the opening whistle. **Marcus Bell** opened the scoring after just three minutes, capitalising on a defensive error from the Ginge FC backline. Bell doubled the lead in the seventh minute with a composed finish — and it could have been more before the half time whistle.

AngryGinge's side showed some fight through **Sam Adeyemi**, who pulled one back in the twelfth minute with a powerful strike from the right side of the box. But within 90 seconds, Yanited restored their two-goal advantage through Bell's hat-trick goal — a superb left-footed curler into the top corner.

**Half time: Yanited 3-1 Ginge FC**

## Second Half

The second half began with Ginge FC pushing forward desperately looking for a route back into the game. They found it through **Adeyemi again** — his second of the match, a bullet header at the far post.

But Yanited were simply too clinical on the night. Two further goals — one from a penalty after what would have been a corner kick, and a breakaway goal in the closing minutes — ensured all three points stayed at the top of the table.

## The Game Changer

The four-goal gap when the **Game Changer** period began meant Yanited could afford to sit back and see out the match. Despite the 3v3 format and double goals rule, Ginge FC couldn't find the net — the match ended at 5-2 on the full-time whistle.

## Talking Points

- **Marcus Bell** is now on 14 goals for the season — three ahead of his nearest rival Rico Chambers in the golden boot race
- Yanited have now won six of their eight matches and are strong favourites for the Final Four
- AngryGinge was gracious in defeat but admitted his side "have to do better defensively" in the remaining games

## What's Next

Yanited face **Alpine FC** in Gameweek 9 — a potentially crucial match as Alpine FC are within touching distance of the playoff spots. Ginge FC face fellow strugglers **W2S Warriors** in what could be a relegation six-pointer.
